About Me

I am a passionate researcher and engineer currently pursuing a PhD in Computer Science at Tufts University's GOLD Lab, advised by Prof. Reuth Mirsky. My current research focuses on applying Reinforcement Learning for Human-Robot Interaction in Rehabilitation.

Previously, I completed my M.Sc. and B.Sc. in Electrical and Electronic Engineering at Tel Aviv University, graduating Magna Cum Laude for my Bachelor's. My Master's thesis explored "Advances in analysis and control of synchronverter," reflecting my interest in power systems and control theory. I have experience spanning academic research, software engineering, and application engineering in diverse fields like robotics, AI, power systems, and semiconductor manufacturing.
