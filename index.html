About Me

I am a PhD student in Computer Science at Tufts University, working in the GOLD Lab under the mentorship of Assistant Professor Reuth Mirsky. My research focuses on Reinforcement Learning for Human-Robot Interaction in Rehabilitation.

With a strong background in Electrical and Electronic Engineering from Tel Aviv University, I've worked extensively on innovative projects in power systems, particularly advancing the analysis and control of synchronverters.

My professional experience spans research, system engineering, and consultancy roles across multiple countries. I'm passionate about applying machine learning and AI to solve real-world problems, especially in robotics and human-computer interaction.
